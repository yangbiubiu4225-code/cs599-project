# Music Appreciation Guidelines

## Tempo And Groove

Keywords: tempo, groove, rhythm, BPM, 节奏, 律动, 速度, 鼓点, 紧凑。

Scoring guidance: compare detected tempo with the target tempo from Music Spec. A close tempo match supports intent alignment. Faster tempo and stronger onset activity often create tension or drive; slower tempo and softer attacks create calm, spacious, or reflective feeling.

Revision guidance: if the track feels slow for an energetic target, shorten the intro and introduce percussion earlier. If it feels too dense, reduce transient layers and leave more breathing space.

## Energy And Dynamics

Keywords: energy, dynamics, rms, loudness, 能量, 动态, 响度, 层次。

Scoring guidance: RMS energy reflects overall loudness and density. High energy can support action, trailer, or dance scenes, but can feel crowded for study or meditation. Low energy can support calm scenes but may feel weak for cinematic impact.

Revision guidance: use stronger bass and percussion to increase energy; reduce continuous layers and add section contrast to lower density.

## Timbre And Brightness

Keywords: timbre, brightness, spectral centroid, texture, 音色, 明亮度, 频谱, 质感。

Scoring guidance: spectral centroid helps describe brightness. Higher brightness suggests sharper, clearer, more foreground sound; lower brightness suggests warm, dark, or background texture. The target depends on mood and scene.

Revision guidance: add bright synth lead, shimmer pad, or high-frequency accents for more clarity; use warmer midrange pads and softer highs when brightness is excessive.

## Motif And Memorability

Keywords: motif, hook, theme, melody, 主旋律, 动机, 主题, 记忆点。

Scoring guidance: a strong motif should repeat with slight variation, stay in the foreground, and remain recognizable after one listening pass. Dense accompaniment can weaken memorability.

Revision guidance: create a repeated two-bar motif, reduce competing accompaniment, and return to the hook near the ending.
